# AmiSafe — Ethics Code

All contributors and users of AmiSafe agree to the following principles,
aligned with the Africa AI Safety Prize 2026 Code of Conduct.

## 1. Do no harm
AmiSafe exists to surface and reduce AI-related harms. No feature, deployment,
or use of its data should introduce new harms. If a contribution could harm
reporters, communities, or individuals, it must not be merged without explicit
mitigation.

## 2. Privacy is non-negotiable
No personal identifier — name, phone number, email, IP address, device fingerprint,
or location — shall ever be stored in association with a report without the
informed, explicit, and revocable consent of the reporter.

## 3. Community ownership
Data submitted by communities belongs to those communities. AmiSafe is a
steward, not an owner. Aggregate intelligence derived from community reports
must be made available to the communities that produced it.

## 4. Transparency
All code is open source. All data processing pipelines are documented.
All limitations, assumptions, and failure modes are honestly disclosed in
project documentation.

## 5. No weaponisation
AmiSafe must not be used to target individuals, facilitate surveillance,
support authoritarian enforcement, or suppress legitimate speech.
Any evidence of such use must be reported immediately to the maintainers.

## 6. Responsible disclosure
Security vulnerabilities, data breaches, or privacy failures must be
disclosed to maintainers within 48 hours of discovery via
security@amisafe.org (PGP key: [to be added]).

## 7. Inclusion
Contributions must not discriminate on the basis of language, ethnicity,
religion, gender, disability, or national origin. All African language
communities are equally valued.

---

Violations of this code may result in removal of contributor access.
